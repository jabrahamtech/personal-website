**Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet**, consectetur adipiscing elit. Insurance brokers receive quote requests as a chaotic mix of forwarded emails, PDF attachments, scanned forms, and free-text notes. The job of turning that mess into a clean record is high-volume, low-margin, and historically resistant to automation.

This is a representative breakdown of a workflow I designed for a broker-side intake team. *Specifics anonymised*; the architecture is the lesson.

## The problem

Sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua. Each new quote required ~12 minutes of manual data entry across three systems, with a 7% rate of missing fields that triggered a back-and-forth email cycle averaging 36 hours.

## Architecture

- **Inbox watcher** — IMAP + S3 staging; every artifact gets a content hash and a permanent URI.
- **Type router** — small classifier decides email-body / PDF / scan / note; routes to the right extractor.
- **Extraction** — schema-bound LLM calls per type, with confidence per field.
- **Missing-info checker** — deterministic pass over the schema; auto-drafts a clarification email for review.
- **Human review** — broker UI shows the source artifact next to the extracted record; approve / edit / reject.
- **CRM handoff** — idempotent writer with retries; every field has provenance.
