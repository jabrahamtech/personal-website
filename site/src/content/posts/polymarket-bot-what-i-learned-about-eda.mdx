This is an upcoming draft in the content pipeline.

## Draft angle

The post will use a Polymarket bot as a practical way to explain event-driven architecture. Prediction-market data changes constantly, but most of the system should react to events instead of polling everything and hoping the timing works out.

<Callout type="tip">
  The useful lesson is not the bot itself. It is the boundary between market data ingestion, event normalization, strategy decisions, and order execution.
</Callout>

## Architecture sketch

The draft will separate the system into small pieces with clear contracts: ingest events, normalize them, make a decision, place an order, and record what happened.

## Failure modes

<Callout type="warn">
  Bots fail fastest when the event log cannot explain what decision was made from which market state.
</Callout>

The post will cover stale prices, duplicate events, rate limits, partial fills, and why every strategy decision needs enough context to be replayed later.
