This is an upcoming draft in the content pipeline.

## Draft angle

AI is starting to "sound like you" because products are learning from the small signals you create every day: edits, prompts, documents, preferences, rejected suggestions, accepted suggestions, workflows, and repeated phrasing.

The post will focus on the micro-training layer behind personalised AI. Not one big training run that turns a model into a clone, but constant adaptation around your data and behaviour until the tool starts predicting how you would write, decide, route work, and respond.

## Intended reader

This is for founders, operators, and knowledge workers trying to understand why AI products are becoming more personalised, what data they are using to get there, and where the boundary sits between useful adaptation and uncomfortable surveillance.
