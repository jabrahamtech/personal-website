Insurance intake is the textbook case for AI workflow automation: high-volume, low-margin, structurally repetitive, and yet stubbornly resistant to off-the-shelf tools. The reason is that the inputs are messy — forwarded email chains, PDF schedules, scanned forms, free-text broker notes — and the output has to be clean enough to enter into a regulated system of record.

Here's how I'd actually design the workflow, end to end.

## 1. Get a single source-of-truth for inputs

Sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua. Before any AI, every artifact lands in a content-addressed object store with a permanent URI. Every downstream extraction links back to that URI plus a byte range. No file lives anywhere else.

## 2. Route by type, not by intent

A small classifier decides "email body / PDF / scan / note" and routes to a type-specific extractor. Don't ask one giant prompt to handle all four — it'll be mediocre at all of them.

## 5. Treat human review as the product

## 6. Handoff is its own system
